  • Wear a mask if you are coughing or sneezing
  • Masks are effective only when used in combination with frequent hand-cleaning with alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water
  • If you wear a mask then you must know how to use and dispose of it safely and correctly
  • Before putting on a mask, clean hands with alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water
  • Cover mouth and nose with mask and make sure there are no gaps between your face and the mask
  • Avoid touching the mask while using it; On certain events when you do, make sure to do it with clean hands as directed prior
  • Replace the mask with a new one as soon as it is damp and do not re-use single-use masks
  • To remove the mask
    • Remove it from behind using the strings hooked behind your ear
    • Discard immediately in a closed bin
    • Wash your hands as directed

Researchers show that the virus can stay for a few hours until a few days. The duration itself can be affected by external factors such as the surface type and temperature. As of now, there is no evidence that shows that the coronavirus can be spread from mosquito bites. This virus is a respiratory virus that spreads through cough droplets or breaths of those who have been infected. To protect yourself, make sure to wash your hands frequently with soap and running water or alcohol-based hand rub. Furthermore, avoid contact from those who are showing the coronavirus symptoms.
The virus shows symptoms in the span of 1-14 days. As a result, people who are suspected to have the virus are recommended to stay isolated for 14 days in their own homes, hospitals, or locations where tracking these symptoms are made easy. In order to confirm if an individual caught the virus, they can take frequent tests. Throughout isolation, individuals must follow all the instructions from healthcare workers to minimize the spread of the virus.
